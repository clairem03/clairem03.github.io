The opportunity

Buying beef in bulk is harder than it should be

Buying meat directly from local farms means better quality and a fairer deal for the farmer — but it's confusing for a first-time buyer. Cuts, quantities, pricing by weight, and pickup logistics all sit outside what most people know how to navigate.

As a co-founder I set out to make that whole path approachable: help a consumer go from curious to confident, and give farmers a straightforward way to sell in volume. I led the user research, shaped the product, and built toward an MVP.

Reflection

What I took from it

  • Starting from a problem we owned meant every design decision traced back to a real user need, not a spec.
  • Wearing the research, product, and frontend hats at once taught me to scope ruthlessly — ship the thing that answers the riskiest question first.
  • Talking to both sides of a marketplace early saved us from building the wrong thing for either.
  • Add a real outcome — early traction, a validated assumption, or what's next.
